Engr. Vince Jebryl Montero

April 1 & 29, 2023

Session Description

Embedded systems are computer systems designed to perform specific tasks as part of larger systems. They are commonly found in everyday gadgets such as household appliances and automotive systems. In the session, we learned about hardware integration in embedded systems and its advantages and limitations. We also had a practical activity where we programmed a minicomputer. Hardware integration in embedded systems involves the close integration of various hardware components to meet specific application requirements. These components include microcontrollers or microprocessors, memory units, input/output interfaces, and sensors or actuators. In the practical activity, we programmed a minicomputer to display the word "HELLO" on a 7-segment display using the minicomputer's programming language. This activity provided hands-on experience in implementing a basic function of an embedded system.

Reflection

The embedded systems course was an excellent resource, providing a wealth of engaging and valuable information for anyone interested in the field. The skills and knowledge gained from this course are particularly important given the high demand for expertise in this area of computer engineering. As a refresher course, it complemented my previous studies in embedded systems during my third year, enhancing my understanding of programming aspects related to this field. The highlight of the course was a challenging task that required us to display the word "HELLO" on the 7-segment display of the FPGA board using assembly programming. Successfully completing this task greatly boosted my confidence and reinforced my belief in my capabilities. Overall, the course was an enriching experience, offering essential knowledge and practical skills in the field of embedded systems.
